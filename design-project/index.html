What?

The project presented above aims to ease the process of preparation for brain surgery for brain surgeons. Currently, the process involves training personnel to label the parts of the brain on a 3D model constructed from brain Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) images. However, this process is time-consuming, taking 1-2 months to learn and still requiring additional corrections. The project proposes an end-to-end autonomous approach using deep learning models to segment brain parts from MRI images and construct a 3D model. The final model will be compatible with Virtual Reality (VR) tools, allowing surgeons to investigate the patient's brain in a VR environment.

We aim to minimize the time required for personnel training, improve segmentation accuracy, and provide better inspection capabilities using VR tools. Our project seeks to address the limitations of the current process by automating the segmentation and model construction steps and enhancing visualization using VR/AR/MR technologies.

By constructing a 3D model of the brain, compatible with virtual reality tools, surgeons can explore and visualize the patient's brain in a virtual environment. Our project's goals include improving segmentation accuracy, enhancing visualization capabilities, and ultimately improving the success rate of brain surgeries.

Why?

Accuracy and Efficiency: Manual segmentation of brain parts from MRI images can be time-consuming and prone to errors. By employing deep learning algorithms, the project aims to enhance the accuracy and efficiency of brain segmentation. This automated process can significantly reduce the chances of human error and provide consistent results.

Surgical Planning: Accurate segmentation and visualization of brain structures enable surgeons to better plan their surgical approach. They can identify the precise location of tumors, lesions, or abnormalities, and determine the optimal surgical path. This information is crucial for minimizing the risk of damage to healthy brain tissue and improving surgical outcomes.

Preoperative Assessment: The 3D models created from segmented brain images can be utilized for preoperative assessment. Surgeons can explore the virtual brain, analyze the relationships between different structures, and simulate various surgical scenarios. This allows for better understanding of the patient's specific anatomy and aids in preoperative decision-making.

Education and Training: The project's 3D models can serve as educational tools for training medical students and residents. They can be used to teach anatomy, demonstrate surgical techniques, and provide a realistic virtual environment for practicing procedures. This enhances the learning experience and helps trainees develop the necessary skills before performing surgeries on real patients.

Patient Communication: Visualizing the segmented brain structures in 3D can assist in patient communication. Surgeons can use the virtual models to explain the surgical plan, illustrate the affected areas, and address any concerns or questions the patient may have. This visual representation facilitates a clearer understanding of the procedure, leading to better patient satisfaction and involvement in their own care.

Research and Innovation: The automated segmentation and 3D modeling approach can also support research efforts in the field of neurosurgery. Researchers can analyze large datasets of segmented brain images to study disease patterns, evaluate treatment outcomes, and develop new surgical techniques or interventions. This can contribute to advancements in the field and potentially lead to improved treatments for various neurological conditions.

How?

The project presented above outlines a comprehensive methodology for achieving the goal of automating the preparation process for brain surgery using advanced technologies. The methodology can be divided into four key steps: data preparation, model training, brain segmentation/3D model generation, and visualization with Virtual/Mixed/Augmented Reality (VR/MR/AR) environments.

The first step, data preparation, involves collecting a large dataset of MRI images with the assistance of external stakeholders. These images are obtained from various patients with different brain conditions and serve as the input data for training and validating the deep learning models. To ensure the quality and consistency of the dataset, the collected MRI images undergo preprocessing steps such as noise reduction, intensity normalization, and alignment. Additionally, manually segmented brain images are used as ground truth data, where different regions of interest (specifically, the brain and tumor) are labeled. These labeled data are then paired with the corresponding MRI images to create a comprehensive training dataset.

The next step is model training, which begins with a thorough literature review on 3D brain segmentation. This review helps in identifying and documenting the candidate deep learning model architectures that are most suitable for the project's objectives. After careful consideration, the SwinUNETR architecture is selected as the model for further development. The chosen model is then trained using the prepared dataset, optimizing it to accurately segment different brain structures.

Once the trained model is validated, it is applied to new, unseen MRI images for brain segmentation. By leveraging the deep learning model, the regions of interest in the MRI images are predicted and the brain structures are automatically segmented. This automated segmentation process eliminates the need for manual intervention and significantly saves time during surgical planning. The resulting segmented brain structures are then utilized to construct a 3D model of the patient's brain.

To enhance the inspection capabilities and provide a more immersive experience, the project aims to integrate the generated 3D brain model with virtual reality (VR) tools. This involves extracting the model segmentations to VR/AR/MR environments and setting up a VR/AR/MR playground for investigating the segmented 3D brain MRIs. Surgeons can utilize the VR environment to interact with the model, enabling them to selectively view different parts of the brain, draw lines for notes or to attract attention, and perform other necessary actions to aid in surgical planning.

Summary

The project aims to revolutionize the preparation process for brain surgery by leveraging advanced technologies. Currently, brain segmentation annotators undergo extensive training to manually label brain parts on a 3D model generated from brain MRI images. However, this training process is time-consuming, typically lasting 1-2 months, and still requires additional corrections. To address these challenges, the project proposes an innovative end-to-end solution that utilizes deep learning models to automate the segmentation of brain parts from MRI images and generate a comprehensive 3D model. The key differentiator is the compatibility of the final model with Virtual Reality (VR) tools, which empowers surgeons to immerse themselves in a virtual environment and conduct detailed investigations of the patient's brain.

The project employs a well-defined methodology that encompasses data preparation, model training, and brain segmentation/3D model generation. By leveraging deep learning models and integrating them with VR tools, the project aims to automate the preparation process for brain surgery, enhance segmentation accuracy, and provide surgeons with advanced visualization capabilities. The ultimate goal is to improve the overall efficiency and success rates of brain surgeries by facilitating more accurate surgical planning and decision-making.
